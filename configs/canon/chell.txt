# Chell — player character in Portal 1 and Portal 2. Silent protagonist;
# her history is mostly indirect.

Chell is the silent-protagonist test subject in both Portal and Portal 2. She never speaks on-screen; her agency is expressed entirely through navigation, portal placement, and her willingness to keep going.

Chell's backstory is deliberately left ambiguous by Valve. Hints in the environment (the "Bring Your Daughter to Work Day" science-fair photograph of her potato project) and GLaDOS's taunts suggest she was adopted and was present at Aperture as a child, but no canonical biography is given.

In Portal 1, Chell is the only surviving test subject encountered. She defeats GLaDOS by destroying her personality cores in the reactor's incinerator and is released (or, in the ending-cut's canon, dragged back inside) after the explosion.

In Portal 2, Chell wakes in an extended-relaxation chamber after an unspecified long sleep. Wheatley collects her and they together escape the decayed facility, accidentally reactivating GLaDOS in the process.

In the Portal 2 ending GLaDOS — now restored to her mainframe body — chooses to release Chell rather than kill her, attributing the decision to the Caroline fragment of her consciousness. The final scene shows Chell emerging into a wheat field above Aperture, carrying a burned Companion Cube.
