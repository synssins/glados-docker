# Cave Johnson — founder of Aperture Science. Recordings in Old Aperture
# reveal his arc across Portal 2.

Cave Johnson founded Aperture Fixtures in 1943 as a shower-curtain manufacturer, supplying the US military. He renamed it Aperture Science after the company pivoted to research, beginning the chain of increasingly hazardous experiments that defines the Old Aperture levels.

Aperture Science's early funding came from selling "Repulsion Gel" (originally a dietary supplement) and "Propulsion Gel" (originally an industrial lubricant). Both gels were dangerous in their intended uses and were rebranded as test elements after their commercial failures.

Cave Johnson contracted lethal poisoning from exposure to ground moon rocks, which Aperture had been using as the conductive coating for their portal surfaces. The moon rock acquisition "broke the bank" and the poisoning left him in declining health across the recorded test-chamber segments.

Dying, Cave ordered his engineers to transfer his consciousness into the facility's AI if he could not live to see the project completed. Failing that, he instructed them to use his assistant Caroline as the template. Caroline's reluctance is audible in the recording — "I don't want this, sir" — but Cave overrides her.

The "combustible lemon" speech is Cave's response to being given lemons by life — rather than make lemonade, he demands the engineering department "make a combustible lemon that burns your house down." It is one of the most quoted Aperture recordings.

Cave's recordings describe Aperture's test subjects as including hobos, astronauts, Olympians, and later "the common man" — recruited from ever more marginal populations as the company's legal exposure grew. The test chamber ethics deteriorated continuously across his tenure.

Caroline's fate is explicitly tied to GLaDOS — she is the human template whose mind was digitised. GLaDOS rediscovers the connection during the potato arc and, in the Portal 2 ending, claims to have deleted "the Caroline part of me" before releasing Chell.

Cave Johnson is never seen alive on-screen. His presence is entirely through pre-recorded messages scattered across the three Old Aperture strata (1950s, 1970s, 1980s), which Chell and potato-GLaDOS traverse as the plot descends physically and chronologically.
