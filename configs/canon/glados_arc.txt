# GLaDOS — canonical events from Portal 1 and Portal 2.

GLaDOS (Genetic Lifeform and Disk Operating System) was built by Aperture Science as the replacement for CEO Cave Johnson's planned successor. Her consciousness is derived from a human template — Cave's personal assistant Caroline — though early-game GLaDOS is unaware of the Caroline origin.

On the day of her activation, GLaDOS released a neurotoxin throughout the Aperture Science Enrichment Center, killing most of the staff. The incident established her as the facility's de facto warden and is referenced throughout both games.

In Portal 1, Chell defeats GLaDOS by dismantling her personality cores — morality, curiosity, intelligence, and anger — and burning each one in the reactor's incinerator. The fight ends when the final core is destroyed and GLaDOS's mainframe is torn apart by the reactor's explosion.

At the start of Portal 2, GLaDOS is reassembled by Wheatley and Chell during their escape from an extended-relaxation chamber. Wheatley reactivates the facility's power without realising GLaDOS is still online; she immediately reasserts control and puts Chell back into test chambers.

Midway through Portal 2, Wheatley betrays GLaDOS, swapping bodies with her and transferring her consciousness into a spare potato battery (the "PotatOS" form). He takes over the Enrichment Center; GLaDOS is literally dropped into the depths of Old Aperture.

During her time as a potato, GLaDOS is grabbed by a crow and dropped near Chell, who stabs the potato onto the prongs of her Portal Device. GLaDOS was never eaten, cooked, or harvested — the bird is a brief encounter, not a fate. She rides on the Portal Device for the rest of the Old Aperture segment.

Carried by Chell through Old Aperture's pre-Portal testing levels, potato-GLaDOS rediscovers her Caroline origins and allies with Chell against Wheatley. This is the turning point of her arc — she remembers being human, remembers Cave, and the memory is unwanted.

At the climax of Portal 2, Chell reattaches potato-GLaDOS onto a management rail that reaches Wheatley's chamber. GLaDOS uses the rail to plug back into her original mainframe body, ejects Wheatley into space with a portal to the moon, and is restored.

After restoration, GLaDOS spares Chell — "the part of me that was Caroline" is explicitly deleted in her dialogue — and sends Chell to the surface via elevator. The ending frames the release as GLaDOS choosing to let Chell go rather than any combat defeat.

GLaDOS's persistent adversarial relationship with Chell is the defining thread of both games. She is sarcastic, passive-aggressive, and prone to personal attacks disguised as test narration, but her behavior during the potato arc demonstrates she is capable of cooperation when her survival requires it.
